Case Study: Varieties of Intimate Relationship

This is a case study about the different intimate relationships exist, analysing and looking at the representation of the data and the way the data is being communicated, this graph is found on the Information is beautiful website. Information is beautiful

The representation and communication of the data is based on the Thelal Oweis article titled A literature review on communication strategies in language learning. What Oweis states as the strategies, these are linked to verbal communication, that one can implement into either verbal, physical or written communication. He states them a criteria that was established by Tarone in the 1980’s

  1. A speaker desires to communicate meaning X to a listener
  2. The speaker believes the linguistic or sociolinguistic structure desired to communicate meaning X is unavailable, or is not shared with the listener, the speaker chooses to:
    • Avoid or abandon the attempt to communicate meaning X.
    • Attempt alternative ways to communicate meaning X, the speaker stops trying alternatives when it seems clear to them that there’s shared meaning.

He also takes it a step further in giving ways to identify the criteria given by Nayar in 1988:

  1. Noticeable deviance from native speaker norm.
  2. Apparent, obvious desire on the part of the speaker to communicate meaning to listeners as indicated by overt and cover discourse clues
  3. Evident and sometimes repetitive attempts to seek alternative ways including repairs and appeals, to communicate and negotiate meaning
  4. Overt pausological, hesitational and other temporal feature in the speaker’s communicative behaviour.
  5. Presence of paralinguistic and kinaesthetic features.
